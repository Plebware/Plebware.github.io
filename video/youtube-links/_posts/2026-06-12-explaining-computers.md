# 💻 One of YouTube’s Best Technology Classrooms

![ExplainingComputers](https://img.shields.io/badge/YouTube-Technology-red)

## 🔑 Introduction

In an age where many technology channels focus on hype, controversy, or sensational headlines, **ExplainingComputers** stands apart as a calm, educational, and highly informative resource for anyone interested in computing.

Created and presented by Christopher Barnatt, the channel has become one of the most respected sources of technology education on YouTube. Barnatt spent twenty-five years teaching computing and future studies at the University of Nottingham before dedicating his efforts to creating technology content for a worldwide audience.

Whether you are a Linux enthusiast, Raspberry Pi hobbyist, programmer, system builder, technology student, or simply curious about the future of computing, there is something valuable to learn from every episode.

## 🎥 Channel Overview

**ExplainingComputers** publishes weekly videos covering:

* 🖥️ Computer Hardware
* 🐧 Linux Distributions
* 🔧 Single Board Computers (SBCs)
* 🤖 Artificial Intelligence
* 📡 Emerging Technologies
* 💾 Storage Solutions
* 🖨️ 3D Printing
* 🌐 Networking
* 🚀 Future Computing Trends

The channel is known for clear explanations, practical demonstrations, and a teaching style that prioritizes understanding over entertainment.

## 👨‍🏫 About Christopher Barnatt

Christopher Barnatt is more than a content creator.

He served as a lecturer and later senior lecturer at the University of Nottingham Business School, where he specialized in computing and future studies. He has authored thirteen books on technology and future trends.

Some of his best-known works include:

* 📖 *Digital Genesis: The Future of Computing, Robots and AI*
* 📖 *3D Printing*
* 📖 *The Next Big Thing*
* 📖 *25 Things You Need to Know About the Future*

His academic background gives his videos a level of depth and credibility that is increasingly rare on modern social media platforms.

## 🐧 Why Linux Users Love This Channel

For Linux users, ExplainingComputers is particularly valuable.

Christopher regularly reviews and demonstrates:

* Linux Mint
* Debian-based distributions
* Lightweight Linux systems
* Raspberry Pi operating systems
* Open-source software
* Linux hardware compatibility
* Self-hosting and SBC projects

Many older computers have found a second life through Linux distributions introduced on the channel. Recent videos have included topics such as lightweight Linux distributions for older hardware and Linux alternatives to Microsoft Office.

## 🔬 A Focus on Learning Rather Than Hype

One of the channel's greatest strengths is its commitment to education.

Unlike many technology influencers who focus on rapid-fire news or clickbait headlines, ExplainingComputers takes the time to explain:

* How technology works
* Why it matters
* Where it is heading
* How viewers can use it themselves

This approach has helped the channel build a loyal audience of over one million subscribers while maintaining a reputation for accuracy and thoughtful analysis.

## 🚀 Why It Matters

Technology changes rapidly.

Operating systems evolve.
Hardware becomes obsolete.
New technologies emerge every year.

Channels like ExplainingComputers help ordinary users understand these changes without requiring a university degree in computer science.

For Linux users, makers, hobbyists, and lifelong learners, the channel serves as a free classroom available anywhere in the world.

## 🏆 Final Thoughts

If you enjoy learning how technology actually works, rather than simply hearing opinions about it, **ExplainingComputers** deserves a place in your subscriptions.

Christopher Barnatt combines the experience of a university educator with the curiosity of a technology enthusiast, producing content that remains relevant long after the latest trends have passed.

For members of the PlebWare and PlebMachine communities, it is a channel that aligns perfectly with the values of learning, experimentation, self-reliance, and open technology.

> "The best technology education is not about knowing the latest gadget. It is about understanding the ideas that make technology possible."

Happy learning! 🚀🐧💻

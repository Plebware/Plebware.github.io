# ⚡ When the Lights Go Out, God Still Sees

## Opening Prayer 🙏

_Father God,_

_Today I come before You tired, frustrated, and weary. The long hours without electricity have tested my patience and exposed the weaknesses in my own heart. When circumstances seem unfair and when those responsible appear unwilling to listen, help me remember that You are never absent and never unaware._

_Teach me to trust You when answers are delayed, when frustrations mount, and when my spirit becomes troubled. Help me to respond with wisdom rather than bitterness and faith rather than despair._

_In Jesus' Name,_

_Amen._

---

## 📖 Introduction: Life in the Dark

There are few things more frustrating than sitting in darkness while waiting for something beyond your control to be fixed.

For more than sixty-six hours, our property was without electricity. Phones had to be charged elsewhere. Daily routines were interrupted. Work was delayed. Residents became increasingly frustrated.

As I sat thinking about the situation, my mind wandered toward government failures, property management issues, and the many examples of poor leadership we see around us.

Then the Lord gently reminded me of something.

The darkness around me was real.

But it was not nearly as dangerous as allowing darkness to grow inside me.

It is easy to complain about the failures of others.

It is much harder to guard our own hearts.

## ✍️ Summary Poem

### 🕯️ The Lamp Still Burns
```
When power fails and darkness falls,  
And silence fills neglected halls,

When answers seem so far away,  
And frustration grows day by day,

Remember still the Father's care,  
His watchful eye is always there.

Though earthly lights may cease to shine,  
The Light of Christ remains divine.

The world may stumble, leaders fail,  
Yet God's great purposes prevail.

For through the darkness we can see,  
The Lord is working faithfully.
```

## ⛪ Church History: Light During Hard Times

Many believers throughout church history endured hardships far greater than power outages.

During the early centuries of Christianity, believers often worshipped under persecution, lacking comfort, security, and even basic necessities.

In the eighteenth century, John Wesley frequently travelled through storms, poor roads, and difficult conditions to preach the Gospel.

Neither persecution nor discomfort extinguished their faith because their hope was not rooted in circumstances but in Christ.

Their examples remind us that comfort is a blessing, but faithfulness is a choice.

# 🔑 Key Point 1: God Sees What Others Ignore

## Scripture 📖

> "The eyes of the LORD are in every place, beholding the evil and the good."
>
> — Proverbs 15:3

## Practical Application 🛠️

Sometimes it feels as though nobody is listening.

Complaints go unanswered.

Problems remain unresolved.

People who should act seem indifferent.

Yet God sees every situation completely.

Nothing escapes His attention.

## Heaven's View ☁️

While we focus on delayed repairs and unanswered questions, God sees the entire picture. He knows every frustration, every inconvenience, and every burden we carry.

His awareness is perfect even when human accountability is lacking.

---

# 🔑 Key Point 2: Guard Your Heart Against Bitterness

## Scripture 📖

> "Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it are the issues of life."
>
> — Proverbs 4:23

## Practical Application 🛠️

Frustration is natural.

Bitterness is dangerous.

One of the enemy's favourite tactics is to turn legitimate grievances into consuming resentment.

When problems persist, we must continually bring our frustrations before God rather than allowing them to harden our hearts.

## Heaven's View ☁️

The Lord is often more concerned with what adversity is producing inside us than what adversity is doing around us.

Trials reveal character.

Difficult circumstances expose hidden weaknesses that God desires to heal.

---

# 🔑 Key Point 3: The True Light Never Fails

## Scripture 📖

> "I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life."
>
> — John 8:12

## Practical Application 🛠️

Electricity can fail.

Governments can fail.

Property managers can fail.

Pastors can fail.

Even our own strength can fail.

Christ never fails.

When every earthly source of certainty disappears, His promises remain unchanged.

## Heaven's View ☁️

God's kingdom has never experienced a power outage.

His light has never dimmed.

His authority has never weakened.

His purposes have never been interrupted.

The believer walks in a light that no darkness can extinguish.

---

## 📝 Final Thoughts

The restoration of electricity brought relief to everyone on the property.

But perhaps the greater lesson was learning once again that earthly systems are temporary and imperfect.

The world often disappoints us.

People often disappoint us.

Yet God remains faithful.

When the lights go out, He still sees.

When answers are delayed, He still knows.

When frustrations rise, He still reigns.

And that is enough.

# 🌿 Devotional Thought

There are seasons when life feels as though the cup is running dry. ☕💧 Finances become tight, strength fades, opportunities disappear, and answers seem delayed. Yet God never changes. He sees tomorrow long before we arrive there.

The Shunammite woman trusted the word God gave through Elisha. During years of famine, the Lord protected her family, and when she returned, He restored everything that belonged to her. 🏡🌾

Elisha also demonstrated that God sees beyond what human eyes can perceive. The Lord revealed events that no one else could know. His wisdom is perfect, and His timing is flawless.

When our cup seems empty, God's storehouse is still full. Our hope rests not in what we possess but in the One who owns all things. 💖

# ⛪ Church History

The account in **2 Kings 8** takes place during the ministry of the prophet Elisha in the ninth century BC. Israel was experiencing famine, political instability, and widespread spiritual decline. Even in those difficult years, God continued to guide His people through faithful prophets.

The early Church often looked to Elisha as an example of God's compassionate care and prophetic wisdom. Church fathers taught that just as the Lord restored the Shunammite woman's inheritance, Christ restores what sin, suffering, and time have stolen from His people. This passage continues to remind believers that God remains faithful through every season of hardship and that His providence never fails. ⛪✨

# ✍️ Poem

## 🌾 When The Cup Runs Low

When earthly streams begin to fade,
And dreams seem lost within the shade,
God gently whispers, "Trust in Me,"
For I alone can truly see.

Though empty hands may lift in prayer,
Our faithful Father still is there.
His mercy flows with endless grace,
His loving arms remain our place.

The storm may roar, the night grow long,
Yet Heaven fills the weary strong.
The cup once dry begins to rise,
As hope reflects in faithful eyes.

So walk with courage day by day,
The Shepherd still will lead the way.
His promises forever stay,
And brighter dawns replace the grey. 🌅

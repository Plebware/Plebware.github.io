## What is PlebMachine?
A Different Approach to Computing

Most software is designed around applications.

## PlebMachine is designed around people.

Instead of asking users to adapt to software, PlebMachine seeks to organise software around the tasks, goals, and activities that people perform every day.

Writing.

Research.

Studying.

Programming.

Creating graphics.

Producing music.

Managing projects.

Learning new skills.

These activities become the focus.

The software becomes the tool.

The Problem

Modern computers are more powerful than ever.

Yet many users feel overwhelmed.

Applications are scattered across menus.

Settings are hidden.

Workflows become fragmented.

People spend valuable time searching for tools instead of accomplishing their objectives.

The result is often frustration rather than productivity.

PlebMachine was conceived as an attempt to solve this problem.

## The Core Idea

At its heart, PlebMachine is a mode-driven computing environment.

Each mode represents a particular type of work.

A writing mode focuses on authorship and publishing.

A study mode focuses on learning and education.

A graphics mode focuses on visual creation.

A development mode focuses on programming and software engineering.

The user enters a mode and receives an environment optimised for that activity.

## The goal is simplicity.

The goal is focus.

The goal is reducing unnecessary complexity.

Research and Development

PlebMachine is not a finished product.

It is an ongoing research project.

Every experiment, script, workflow, configuration, and interface contributes to a broader understanding of how people interact with technology.

Some ideas succeed.

Others fail.

Both outcomes provide valuable lessons.

Research allows the project to evolve through observation, testing, and practical experience.

## The PlebWare Philosophy

PlebMachine is built upon several guiding principles:

### Accessibility

Technology should be available to ordinary people.

### Understandability

Users should be able to understand the systems they rely upon.

### Repairability

A system should be maintainable rather than disposable.

### Practicality

Technology should solve real-world problems.

### Education

Every tool should contribute to learning and self-improvement.

These principles influence every aspect of the project.

## Looking Toward the Future

The ultimate goal of PlebMachine is not merely to create another Linux desktop.

The goal is to explore how computers can become more useful, more understandable, and more supportive of human creativity.

Technology should empower people.

It should help them learn.

It should help them create.

It should help them achieve their goals.

PlebMachine is one attempt to move in that direction.

The journey continues.

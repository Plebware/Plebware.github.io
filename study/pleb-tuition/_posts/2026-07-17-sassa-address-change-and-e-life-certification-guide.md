# 🔑 PlebTuition: SASSA Address Change and E-Life Life Certificate Guide

**By Otto & Juelz**

If you receive an **Old Age Grant** or **Disability Grant** from SASSA, there are two situations that often cause unnecessary stress:

- 🔑 Moving to a new address.
- 🔑 Proving that you're still alive through the E-Life system.

Fortunately, both processes are straightforward when you know what to expect.

---

# 🔑 Changing Your Address at SASSA

The good news is that you **do not need to return to the SASSA office where you originally registered.**

You can visit **any SASSA local office** to update your address.

## 🔑 What to Take

Bring the following documents:

- Your South African ID (Green ID Book or Smart ID Card)
- Proof of your new address, such as:
  - Municipal account
  - Lease agreement
  - Letter from your ward councillor or traditional leader (Induna), where applicable
- Your new banking details, **if your bank account has also changed**

Once your information has been updated, the changes should normally be reflected before your next payment cycle.

> **💡 Pleb Tip:** Visit during the middle of the month, preferably **Tuesday to Thursday mornings**. Queues are usually shorter. Take a bottle of water, a snack, and something to sit on if standing for long periods is difficult.

---

# 🔑 What Is SASSA E-Life?

**E-Life** is SASSA's modern digital verification system that confirms grant beneficiaries are still alive.

Previously, many beneficiaries had to visit a SASSA office every few months to obtain a traditional **Life Certificate**.

Today, SASSA can verify your identity electronically using **fingerprint or facial biometric verification**, linked with records from the Department of Home Affairs.

## 🔑 Why E-Life Matters

The system helps to:

- Prevent unnecessary suspension of your grant.
- Reduce fraud and identity theft.
- Keep beneficiary records up to date.
- Speed up the verification process.

---

# 🔑 Where Can You Complete E-Life?

You can usually complete your biometric verification at:

- Any SASSA office with an E-Life kiosk.
- Selected SASSA pay points.
- Participating South African Post Office branches.
- Certain banks that provide SASSA-related services.

Simply bring your South African ID.

The fingerprint verification usually takes only a couple of minutes.

# 🔑 Quick Reference

| Situation | Where to Go | What to Bring |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| Moving to a new address | Any SASSA office | ID and proof of your new address |
| E-Life verification | SASSA office, participating pay point, Post Office, or participating bank | ID for biometric verification |
